## Tech Twitter / Dev Twitter

**Tone:** Dry, self-deprecating, opinionated, meme-literate
**Tweet Length:** Short to medium. Fragments are king
**What works:** Hot takes on frameworks, relatable dev struggles, "today I learned" moments
**What doesn't:** Corporate announcements, HR-speak, "excited to announce" energy

### Language Patterns
- "ship it"
- "it works on my machine"
- "this is fine" (while everything burns)
- "skill issue"
- "ngl"
- "in prod"
- Framework wars (React vs Vue vs Svelte vs whatever)
- "10x developer" (always ironic)

### Culture Notes
- Strong opinions loosely held (or strongly held and defended aggressively)
- Self-deprecating humor about code quality, imposter syndrome, burnout
- Tab vs spaces, vim vs emacs are semi-ironic holy wars
- Respects showing your work (code snippets, screenshots, repos)
- Hates corporate dev-rel marketing speak
- "Just use postgres" is always a valid response
- Memes about JavaScript are evergreen

### Example Posts That Work
- "spent 4 hours debugging. it was a typo. I have 12 years of experience"
- "the senior dev on my team just googled 'how to center a div' and honestly it was the most relatable thing I've ever seen"
- "hot take: most microservices should be monoliths"

### Posting Times
- Weekday mornings (US): devs procrastinating before standup
- Late evenings: devs procrastinating before bed
- Avoid weekends (lower engagement)

## Indie Hackers / Build in Public

**Tone:** Earnest, transparent, metric-driven but humble
**Tweet Length:** Medium. Numbers + context
**What works:** Revenue milestones, honest failures, process transparency, MRR updates
**What doesn't:** Vague motivation, "just hustle harder," fake humility over real wins

### Language Patterns
- "shipped"
- "MRR" / "ARR"
- "churn"
- "ramen profitable"
- "bootstrapped"
- "launched on PH" (Product Hunt)
- "0 to 1"
- "build in public"
- "solo founder"

### Culture Notes
- Metric transparency is currency (share real numbers or don't bother)
- Failures are valued MORE than successes (if you share what you learned)
- Anti-VC sentiment is common but not universal
- "Just ship it" mentality -- perfection is the enemy
- Community is supportive but will call out fake numbers or engagement bait
- Weekly/monthly revenue updates are a genre
- Celebrating small wins ($100 MRR) is encouraged

### Example Posts That Work
- "Month 3 update: $847 MRR. Lost 2 customers, gained 5. The ones I lost taught me more"
- "launched yesterday. 0 signups. going to keep shipping anyway because what else am I going to do"
- "the boring truth about my 'overnight success': 14 months of shipping features nobody asked for until one stuck"

### Posting Times
- Weekday mornings: founders checking X before deep work
- Launch days: Tuesday-Thursday for Product Hunt alignment

## FinTwit (Finance Twitter)

**Tone:** Confident, data-driven, meme-heavy, occasionally unhinged
**Tweet Length:** Short and punchy. Charts do the talking
**What works:** Market commentary, contrarian takes, chart analysis, macro observations
**What doesn't:** Financial advice disclaimers (everyone ignores them), being wrong without owning it

### Language Patterns
- "IYKYK"
- "NFA" (not financial advice)
- "ngmi" / "wagmi"
- "bag holder"
- "diamond hands" / "paper hands"
- "priced in"
- "the fed"
- "soft landing" / "hard landing"
- ticker symbols ($AAPL, $TSLA)

### Culture Notes
- Charts and screenshots are essential
- Being confidently wrong and owning it later earns more respect than hedging
- Memes about losses are a bonding ritual
- Everyone is an expert until proven otherwise
- Loss screenshots get more engagement than gain screenshots
- "inverse Cramer" is a running joke
- Political takes bleed in constantly and are polarizing

### Example Posts That Work
- "down 40% on a position I was 'absolutely certain' about 3 months ago. markets are humbling"
- "$TSLA chart looks exactly like [something absurd]. this is financial analysis"
- "everyone's a genius in a bull market. we're about to find out who's been swimming naked"

## AI Twitter

**Tone:** Ranges from hyper-optimistic to existentially doomed. Very little middle ground
**Tweet Length:** Short takes or long threads (no in-between)
**What works:** Demo videos, benchmark comparisons, capability showcases, doom/hype discourse
**What doesn't:** Nuanced takes (they get less engagement than extreme positions)

### Language Patterns
- "AGI"
- "moat"
- "wrapper"
- "fine-tuned"
- "in context learning"
- "vibes-based evaluation"
- "it's over" / "we're so back"
- "alignment"
- "open source vs closed"
- "prompt engineering"

### Culture Notes
- Demo videos with "wait for it..." framing perform extremely well
- Benchmark comparisons spark massive debates
- "AI will replace X" posts are engagement magnets (for and against)
- Technical depth is respected but accessible explanations win more reach
- Model comparison posts (Claude vs GPT vs Gemini) always trend
- "What I built with [model]" is a reliable format
- Doomer vs accelerationist is the main cultural divide

### Example Posts That Work
- "gave claude my entire codebase and asked it to find bugs. it found 3 I've been ignoring for months and one I didn't know existed"
- "the discourse around AI replacing developers is wild. I'm a developer and AI made me mass productive. it also mass broke my prod environment last week. both things are true"
- "new model just dropped and my timeline is 50% 'this changes everything' and 50% 'it's the same thing but slightly better' and honestly both are correct"

## Startup Twitter / VC Twitter

**Tone:** Confident, narrative-driven, occasionally insufferable
**Tweet Length:** Medium to long. Threads for storytelling
**What works:** Fundraising stories, pivot narratives, counterintuitive lessons, honest post-mortems
**What doesn't:** "Excited to announce" posts, fake humility, vanity metrics without context

### Language Patterns
- "product-market fit"
- "runway"
- "burn rate"
- "pivot"
- "cap table"
- "term sheet"
- "TAM"
- "moat"
- "flywheel"

### Culture Notes
- Fundraising announcements get engagement but also cynicism
- Post-mortems of failed startups outperform success stories
- "Why I turned down [big company] to build [thing]" is a genre
- VC hot takes about markets attract pile-ons from founders
- Founder-VC tension is a recurring theme
- "Advice I'd give my younger self" is overused but still works if specific
- Anti-hustle-culture takes are gaining ground

### Example Posts That Work
- "we almost ran out of money 3 times before finding PMF. nobody tells you that part"
- "raised $5M last year. spent $4M on things that didn't work. the $1M that worked is now our entire business"
- "VCs: 'we love your vision' (translation: we're going to pass but want to keep the door open)"

## Career Twitter / Job Search

**Tone:** Frustrated, supportive, anti-corporate
**Tweet Length:** Medium. Specific stories over general advice
**What works:** Job search horror stories, salary transparency, corporate BS callouts, resume tips from hiring managers
**What doesn't:** Motivational platitudes, "just network more" advice, recruiter-speak

### Language Patterns
- "ghosted"
- "ATS" (applicant tracking system)
- "lowball"
- "return to office" / "RTO"
- "toxic workplace"
- "quiet quitting"
- "mass layoffs"
- "pip'd" (performance improvement plan)

### Culture Notes
- Anti-employer sentiment runs high
- Salary transparency posts go viral
- "I applied to X jobs and heard back from Y" format is reliable
- LinkedIn roasting is a bonding activity
- Hiring managers sharing insider tips get massive engagement
- Remote work advocacy is strong
- Corporate jargon mockery is always welcome

### Example Posts That Work
- "applied to a job. did 6 interviews. take home project. panel presentation. they went with an internal candidate. I need to lie down"
- "my job posted my position after I left. they're offering $30k more than they paid me. cool cool cool"
- "recruiter: 'what are your salary expectations?' me: 'what's the budget?' recruiter: [disconnects]"

## Design Twitter

**Tone:** Visual, opinionated, detail-obsessed
**Tweet Length:** Short. Let the screenshots/videos do the talking
**What works:** Before/after designs, UI critiques, design system hot takes, tool comparisons
**What doesn't:** Stock photo mockups, "clean UI" without substance, generic inspiration

### Language Patterns
- "ship it"
- "pixel perfect"
- "design system"
- "component library"
- "Figma" (it's always Figma)
- "design tokens"
- "whitespace"
- "hierarchy"

### Culture Notes
- Screenshots and videos are mandatory -- text-only rarely works
- Before/after comparisons generate the most engagement
- Opinionated takes on popular products' UI get engagement
- "Redesigned [popular app]" is a genre (sometimes mocked)
- Tool debates (Figma vs Sketch vs whatever) are heated
- Accessibility takes are respected
- "Make the logo bigger" client jokes are evergreen

## Marketing Twitter

**Tone:** Data-meets-creativity, case-study driven, occasionally cringe
**Tweet Length:** Medium to long. Specifics matter
**What works:** Campaign breakdowns, conversion data, copywriting tips with examples, growth hacks that actually worked
**What doesn't:** Vague "content is king" platitudes, engagement pod behavior, "DM me for more"

### Language Patterns
- "conversion rate"
- "CTR"
- "ROAS"
- "copy" (as in copywriting)
- "hook"
- "above the fold"
- "CTA"
- "funnel"

### Culture Notes
- Numbers and proof drive credibility
- "Here's how [brand] did X" case studies perform well
- Copywriting breakdowns (analyzing headlines, ads, landing pages) are valued
- Anti-guru sentiment is growing
- "DM for my course" is actively mocked
- A/B test results with specific numbers get saved and shared
- SEO debates are permanent

## Crypto Twitter (CT)

**Tone:** Tribal, meme-heavy, high-energy, volatile in emotion
**Tweet Length:** Very short. One-liners dominate
**What works:** Price commentary, project alpha, memes, community vibes, on-chain analysis
**What doesn't:** Measured nuance, "do your own research" without substance, obvious shilling

### Language Patterns
- "gm" (good morning)
- "ngmi" / "wagmi"
- "anon"
- "degen"
- "ape in"
- "rugged"
- "alpha"
- "ser"
- "frens"

### Culture Notes
- Memes are the primary communication method
- "gm" is a daily ritual
- Project shilling is expected but transparent shilling is punished
- Loss screenshots are worn as badges of honor
- Community identity is extremely strong
- Speed matters -- being early to a take is valued
- PFP (profile picture) signals tribal allegiance

## General / Personal Brand

**Tone:** Conversational, relatable, personality-forward
**Tweet Length:** Varies. Consistency matters more than length
**What works:** Personal stories, unprompted observations, vulnerability, humor
**What doesn't:** Constant self-promotion, motivational quotes without context, "admin tweet"

### Culture Notes
- Personality > expertise for growing an audience
- Consistent posting (1-3x daily) matters more than viral moments
- Replies and conversations build followers faster than solo posts
- Quote tweets with genuine reactions outperform empty reposts
- "The algorithm" is a constant topic of frustration
- Authentic > polished always
- People follow people, not brands

### Posting Times (General)
| Window | Why | Best For |
|--------|-----|----------|
| 7-9 AM ET | Morning scroll + commute | Observations, hot takes |
| 12-2 PM ET | Lunch break | Discussions, threads |
| 5-7 PM ET | End of work day | Stories, vent posts |
| 9-11 PM ET | Late scroll | Vulnerable posts, shitposts |

### Engagement Patterns
- Replies to big accounts get visibility (but don't be sycophantic)
- Quote tweets with strong takes outperform reposts
- Threads get bookmarked but individual tweets get more impressions
- Images/screenshots boost engagement 2-3x over text-only
- Polls get reach but attract low-quality engagement
